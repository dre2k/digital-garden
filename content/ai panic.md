Yo dawg, I heard you like AI so I'm asking AI about how to survive the AI upheaval

No but really, the only way forward is to fully leverage the evaluation side of AI implementation or build something yourself. But increasingly, building something yourself is untenable because there is no technological moat; the moat is proprietary records, expert domain knowledge, integration with existing workflows and systems, an existing user base. meaning, what chance does a nobody like me really have if striking out on his own. 

A proposed solution is hyper customizability ('hyper specific persona'), micro-saas so reduce friction for a user as much as possible (actual use case TBD), API glue (can't agents do that?). 


## The Solo Founder Strategy

If you want to start without a user base or data, the playbook is simple:

1. **Find a Migraine:** Look at your own life, your past jobs, or talk to friends in boring, non-tech industries (plumbers, accountants, local retail, property managers). Find the one digital task they _hate_ doing every week.
    
2. **Build a Single Feature:** Build a tool that does _only_ that one thing perfectly. No feature bloat.
    
3. **Launch in Public Hubs:** You don't need a marketing budget. Launch on Product Hunt, specialized Reddit communities, or directly email 50 people who have that exact job title on LinkedIn.
    

When you solve a specific, painful problem for a tiny group of people, they will stick with you—not because your AI model is proprietary, but because your tool is the only one that understands their day-to-day reality.
