While the central narrative of the book covers the author's grief after tragically losing his friend, there's so much about his tone and prose (?) that resonates with me. It's not that we're similar people, but I can see myself in the way he writes his thoughts out, the way he second guesses and wanders around ideas. And of course all the experiences around Berkeley, walking around campus and the city, the familiar locations sounds smell, the smoking, aspirations. I actually spent 2 years living at the Rapa Nui and judging the photos I even kind of look like the mofo lol 

My college years were marked by deep anxiety. I was mired in self-doubt and self-loathing, which is really such a goddamn shame because it caused me to retreat inwards, awash in loneliness and depression, and ultimately miss so many opportunities for new experiences and growth. But although the loneliness ran deep, reading this book made me realize that maybe I wasn't actually alone, but rather unnecessarily sequestered in my own mind by way of my insecurities. All this to say - why do that to yourself? Write, speak, express freely and unapologetically, it might help others see they're not alone. 

so why not create this public journal space. I also noticed it makes me take notes and organize things differently, just the possibility of others randomly running into it.
