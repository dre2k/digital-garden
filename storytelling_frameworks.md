## 1. For Structuring Arguments (The "How-To" Guides)
These are best when you need to present a clear case or answer a direct question.

*   **PREP (Point $\rightarrow$ Reason $\rightarrow$ Example $\rightarrow$ Point):** The go-to for clarity. State your **main point**, back it with the **reasoning**, prove it with an **example**, and restate your **conclusion**.
    *   *Best for:* Q&A sessions, quick executive summaries.
*   **PEEL (Point $\rightarrow$ Evidence $\rightarrow$ Explanation $\rightarrow$ Link):** Great for academic or detailed writing. Present your **point**, support it with **evidence/data**, explain *how* the evidence supports it, and **link** back to the main argument.
    *   *Best for:* Supporting theses, structured reports.

## 2. For Persuasion & Impact (The "Sell" Guides)
Use these when your goal is to change someone's mind or drive action.

*   **PAS (Problem $\rightarrow$ Agitation $\rightarrow$ Solution):** The classic sales/pitch structure. **Highlight the pain point** ($\text{P}$), **magnify its emotional impact** ($\text{A}$), and present your unique **solution** ($\text{S}$).
    *   *Best for:* Marketing pitches, proposing urgent changes.
*   **AIDA (Attention $\rightarrow$ Interest $\rightarrow$ Desire $\rightarrow$ Action):** Perfect for grabbing immediate focus. **Capture attention**, build enough **interest** to keep listening, create a **desire** for the outcome, and finish with a clear **Call to Action**.
    *   *Best for:* Sales emails, keynote openings.

## 3. For Telling Stories (The "Journey" Guides)
These frameworks guide narrative flow, making your content memorable.

*   **Hero's Journey:** The epic, comprehensive arc of transformation. Character faces a challenge $\rightarrow$ endures trials $\rightarrow$ achieves mastery $\rightarrow$ returns changed.
    *   *Best for:* Long-form narratives (books, major company vision).
*   **Three-Act Structure:** A simple, universal plot framework: **Setup** ($\text{Act I}$) $\rightarrow$ **Confrontation/Rising Action** ($\text{Act II}$) $\rightarrow$ **Resolution** ($\text{Act III}$).
    *   *Best for:* Speeches, short presentations, movie plots.
*   **Story Arc (Exposition $\rightarrow$ Rising Action $\rightarrow$ Climax $\rightarrow$ Resolution):** Ideal for building steady tension toward a peak moment before resolving it.
    *   *Best for:* Any narrative where pacing and suspense are key.
*   **ABT (AND, BUT, THEREFORE)** A simple yet powerful structure for concise storytelling. State the situation (AND). Introduce a problem (BUT). Resolve with THEREFORE
